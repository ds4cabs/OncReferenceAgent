# Why OncReferenceAgent Matters

## The Problem
Oncology drug development is the most competitive arena in biopharma, and AACR / ASCO / ESMO / ASH together publish **tens of thousands of abstracts per year**. The data exists — but no human can read 8,000 abstracts in the two weeks before a meeting. Strategy, BD, translational, and R&D teams react late, miss disruptive signals, and produce inconsistent pre-conference briefs. A truly oncology-aware intelligence system would turn this firehose into a structured competitive map.

## What OncReferenceAgent Does
Continuously ingests oncology conference abstracts; maps each to a rigorous oncology ontology (tumor type via OncoTree / NCIt, target via Open Targets, modality across ADC / bispecific / TCE / radioligand / cell therapy / ctDNA / PROTAC); extracts endpoints (ORR, PFS, OS, HR, p-value); scores novelty and impact; and emits a chapterized scientific intelligence report. Every quantitative claim in the report traces back to a source abstract — a strict QC module enforces this.

## Who Benefits
- **Translational and R&D oncology teams** scoping which signals to follow into the lab
- **Competitive intelligence** mapping competitor pipelines by tumor type and modality
- **Business development** spotting in-licensing opportunities and emerging modality momentum
- **Commercial and brand teams** reading the field before launches
- **Leadership** preparing pre-conference briefings and post-conference debriefs
- **Medical affairs** identifying KOLs and tracking narrative shifts in real time

## Why Now
Three forces converge in 2026: (1) oncology abstract metadata is increasingly structured and downloadable; (2) biomedical NER plus oncology ontologies (OncoTree, NCIt, Open Targets) are now mature enough to enable accurate large-scale extraction; (3) LLMs can synthesize source-grounded chapters reliably **when paired with strict QC** that bars invented numbers. What is missing is the integration glue and the oncology-specific domain layer — exactly what OncReferenceAgent provides.

## Why This Is Higher-Leverage Than a Generic ConferenceAgent
A generic conference summarizer must hedge across cardiology, hepatology, oncology, and BD meetings, so it can never deeply model any one domain. Oncology has unique ontologies, endpoints, and modality taxonomy — modeling these well unlocks far higher signal: novel modality-indication pairs, first-in-class targets, MoA-stratified competitor maps, and "most disruptive abstract" rankings that simply cannot be produced without oncology-aware extraction.

## The Bigger Picture
Pre-conference intelligence today absorbs hundreds of senior-oncologist and strategist hours per major meeting across the industry, and the resulting briefings are inconsistent and often arrive late. A reproducible, source-traceable, oncology-aware agent compresses preparation time by 70%+ and frees scientists and strategists to focus on judgment — what the data *means*. From an intern career-development standpoint, this project builds capability at the intersection of **AI-enabled strategy, translational intelligence, and decision-support systems** — exactly the trajectory pharma is investing in most heavily.
